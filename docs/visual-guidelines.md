# Superprism Visual Guidelines

Use this guide when creating Superprism website imagery, page backgrounds, content graphics, and AI image prompts. The core visual language is luminous, optical, and technical: prism physics rendered as cinematic digital landscapes.

## Core Style

Superprism imagery should feel like a glass prism translated into a computational landscape. It combines optical refraction, neon spectral color, dark space, and precise geometric systems.

The mood is sophisticated, calm, and exploratory rather than playful or chaotic. Images should suggest research, systems, maps, intelligence, and emergent structure without becoming corporate stock art or generic sci-fi.

## Visual Motifs

- Glass prisms, triangular facets, transparent pyramids, refractive edges.
- Spectral light split into red, orange, yellow, green, cyan, blue, violet, and magenta.
- Wireframe grids, topographic surfaces, network meshes, contour-like terrain.
- Horizon lines, low camera angles, vanishing-point perspective.
- Soft atmospheric beams, volumetric glow, lens bloom, faint star-field texture.
- Dark negative space for typography and interface overlays.
- Thin luminous lines with subtle blur, glow falloff, and occasional bright nodes.

## Palette

Base the composition on deep black, midnight blue, and dark violet. Use saturated spectral color as light, not as flat fill.

Primary colors:

- Background: near-black, deep navy, blue-black, violet-black.
- Spectral accents: electric cyan, laser blue, emerald green, yellow, orange, red, magenta, violet.
- Highlights: white-hot or pale cyan at the most intense refraction points.

Avoid muddy pastels, beige neutrals, flat corporate blues, and large opaque color blocks. The rainbow should read as emitted or refracted light, with soft halos and transparent overlaps.

## Composition

Favor wide cinematic layouts for website sections and hero imagery. Keep the main energy near a horizon, central prism, or diagonal light path, with enough open dark space for UI and copy.

Strong composition patterns:

- Low wireframe terrain receding into the distance.
- Central or slightly off-center prism splitting a beam of light.
- Diagonal spectral ribbon crossing a dark field.
- Topographic mesh rising into gentle peaks and valleys.
- Layered translucent light trails flowing over a black background.

Keep compositions ordered. The style can be energetic, but it should not become noisy. Use one dominant focal system per image: prism, landscape, ribbon, or mesh.

## Lighting And Texture

Light should behave optically: refracted, split, bent, diffused, or scattered. Edges can glow, but the underlying forms should remain elegant and precise.

Use:

- Volumetric beams with soft bloom.
- Subtle film grain or star-like particulate texture.
- Transparent glass surfaces with faint internal reflections.
- Light falloff into deep shadow.
- Thin linework that glows against black.

Avoid harsh plastic shine, metallic chrome, cartoon lighting, overly glossy 3D product-render surfaces, and flat vector gradients.

## Prompt Vocabulary

Use these phrases in image prompts:

- "cinematic dark optical landscape"
- "glass prism refraction"
- "spectral light dispersion"
- "neon wireframe topography"
- "luminous mesh terrain"
- "volumetric rainbow beams"
- "transparent faceted glass"
- "deep black negative space"
- "soft bloom and subtle film grain"
- "precise geometric linework"
- "low horizon, wide aspect ratio"
- "refracted caustic color"

## Negative Prompt Guidance

Add constraints like these when generating images:

- No people, characters, hands, faces, or animals.
- No text, logos, watermarks, UI mockups, or charts unless requested.
- No flat vector illustration, cartoon style, clip art, or iconography.
- No generic corporate stock imagery.
- No cluttered cyberpunk city, spaceship, robots, or fantasy objects.
- No beige, pastel, or washed-out color palette.
- No heavy smoke, fire, explosions, or chaotic particle storms.

## Website Usage

- Use the darkest, quietest part of an image behind important copy.
- Crop hero images to preserve horizon depth and spectral focal points.
- Keep UI surfaces restrained so the artwork remains the main atmospheric layer.
- Use overlays sparingly: black or navy scrims can improve legibility without killing the glow.
- Prefer wide assets for full-bleed sections and abstract overlays for smaller content blocks.

## Quality Checklist

Before using a new image, confirm:

- It reads as optical/prismatic, not merely rainbow.
- It has dark negative space available for layout.
- Spectral color appears as emitted or refracted light.
- Geometry is clean and intentional.
- The mood is premium, technical, and calm.
- There is no accidental text, logo, watermark, face, or irrelevant sci-fi object.
